## The data set I chose to explore is the ‘NYC Taxis: A day in the life’ data set. It is a very cool data set that shows (in a very sped up way) the route’s different taxis make throughout their shifts. It shows the routes, and while the different routes they make throughout the night run, it shows the total of how much the taxi driver has made, broken town by the tips they make, and other fees they have to pay. It also shows how many hours they’ve been working and how many passengers they have had throughout their shift. You can change between different taxi’s to see multiple taxi drivers and how much they make and drive throughout a shift. It potentially could be used to analyze across time how much the Uber market has effected the taxi industry and their drivers. [NYC Taxis](https://chriswhong.github.io/nyctaxi/)
### This book is written by Professor Tom Leighton and Dr Marten van Dijk, was written for a course at MIT and is called mathematics for computer science. It is interesting to me because I studied a lot of applied mathematics in my undergraduate degree. I love to see all of the way in which mathematics can be applied throughout other disciplines. In this book it teaches the basic principals of discrete mathematics and explains how to use them within the world of computer science. [Mathematics for Computer Science] (https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-042j-mathematics-for-computer-science-fall-2010/pages/readings/)
